FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about our district.

To enroll your child, visit our Enrollment page or contact our Admissions office at (512) 414-1720. You'll need to provide proof of residence, your child's birth certificate, immunization records, and previous school transcripts if transferring from another district.

Required documents include: proof of residence (utility bill, lease agreement, or mortgage statement), student's birth certificate, immunization records, previous school records/transcripts, and parent/guardian ID. For special education services, please also bring any existing IEP or 504 documents.

Intra-district transfers are available based on space availability. Submit a transfer request through the Admissions office or online portal. Priority is given to students who have siblings at the desired school or whose parents work at the school.

Contact our Special Education Department at (512) 414-1760 or email specialed@austinisd.org. They can help with IEP processes, 504 plans, and connecting you with resources and support services for your child's unique needs.

Visit our Careers page or contact Human Resources at (512) 414-1740. We offer competitive salaries, comprehensive benefits, and professional development opportunities for teachers, administrators, and support staff.

Bus route information is available through the parent portal or by contacting Transportation Services at (512) 414-1770. Routes are typically published one week before the school year begins. Students living more than 2 miles from school are eligible for transportation services.

We offer comprehensive programs including STEM, Advanced Placement courses, fine arts, career and technical education (CTE), dual language immersion, special education services, and early college high school programs. Visit our Academics page for detailed program information.

Visit our district website and click on "Parent Portal" at the top of the page. First-time users will need to create an account using their student's ID number. The portal allows you to view grades, attendance, schedules, and communicate with teachers.

Emergency Information

For immediate emergencies, please call 911. For school-related emergencies during school hours, contact your child's school directly.
